Sensors

  Various sensor types are available for depth estimation. A simple monocular camera is the most basic and cost-effective option. Though stereo, LiDAR, and thermal cameras can be applicable in certain situations, a monocular camera is the most accessible. Many modern smartphones carry at minimum, such a monocular camera. In addition to being an accessible and portable device, mobile phones make an ideal candidate for various depth-reliant applications.

Monocular Cameras

Given that we will rely on a monocular camera, the input of which will be the pixels of an image, the pipeline needs to be built around that. The task therefore is to handle calculating a depth map solely from the data of the visible objects in the image. With respect to the figure, the application needs to infer the right half of the image based off the left half of the image. Notably, monocular cameras do not provide any ground truth depth estimation as other sensors such as LiDAR might. The prediction needs to be made solely based off of the content of the image. Much like a human, the application must rely on visual cues such as shadows to estimate depth.
